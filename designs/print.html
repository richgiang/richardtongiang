The roots of my personal design history
I first started designing in school news organizations and have worked with numerous entities for the past 6 years including the Spark Newsmagazine, Fisher Ink, and The Lantern Newspaper. During my print design career I have served in several editor and managing editor positions, most recently as the Managing Editor for Design for the Lantern Newspaper, the independent student voice at Ohio State University.
The Lantern Newspaper
From concept to print
As an assistant design editor in 2019 I realized that many of our layouts were becoming repetitive and cookie-cutter in addition to being text-heavy and gray. I worked with one of our advisors to rework a template that could elevate everyday designs without reducing our speed and efficiency during print nights. The first spread is was the final prototype and it came to fruition for the first time in the form of the second spread.
Best of OSU
Using what I had learned during the winter Rivalry Edition, I created a special style guide for the Best of OSU edition (the edition features the best parts of Ohio State voted on by the student body). I noticed we had high quality photos and wanted to feature them without flooding the space with too much weight, so I opted for two-page spreads for most of the stories and focused on readability with clean typefaces, weighted anchors and consistent color coordination.
Spur of the Moment
This is a favorite spread of mine that I changed last minute. Initially, I was given a basic photo of the VR headset on a plain background. I decided to maximize playfulness and cut out each element to space out weight for a more minimalistic modern look. This moment taught me how to make the most of particularly flat, uninteresting images.
