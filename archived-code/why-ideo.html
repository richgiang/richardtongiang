IDEO.org's 64 employees and the stories they work on are awe-inspiring
After reading the bios of all 64 creatives at IDEO.org and reading the case studies they've poured empathy, powerful design, and people-centric practices into, I can say that I'm wildly excited about the possibilities of contributing my own story to IDEO.org's work and culture.
I love this website. It's exactly the type of storytelling experience that I'd love to replicate and build on in my work with specific IDEO.org partners and organizations
Why I'm excited

As I've combed through IDEO.org's digital footprint, I've gradually become more and more excited about the possibility of contributing my perspective to IDEO.org's current body of work. In addition, the opportunity to create practices and discover principles to be reproduced and refined in the future gets me excited.

Specifically, the principles of finding hope in human stories, being more powerful together, taking mistakes in stride, and getting a little messy when not knowing all of the answers all really resonate with me and how I've developed my style as a designer and storyteller.

The more I read about perspectives, employee bios, and the impact report, the more alignment I see with how I design and tell stories — it's a bit uncanny.

People I'd love to learn from

If I were to work at IDEO.org I would make it a point to get food with as many people in the organization as I could and learn about their own stories, but these folks stories particularly stuck out to me.

Emily Sadeghian

Emily's article titled "The Material Effects of Stories", ties directly into my design philosophy of producing positive interactions as a result of Expectancy Violations Theory. I'd love to tap into her deep knowledge of storytelling as a writer to help me better formalize my writing practices — something I've been meaning to work on. Her empathetic abilities are evident in her writing, and I'd love to share a conversation over film and vintage store shopping.

Justine Dela Cruz

I listened to the 'Good UX' chat that Justine participated in and got great insight into how IDEO.org works behind the scenes. Her perspective in valuing the supportive nature of everyone in the organization is something I really resonate with and I'd like to learn about what she has learned from the wealth of knowledge she's gathered. I'd also love to learn about her experience with startups since I'm also coming from that world!

Shesley Crustna

Everyone at IDEO.org likely has a worldly perspective due to the nature of the work, but I was particularly drawn to Shesley's mix of background. His work as an artist in Mauritius is incredibly interesting to me and as a fellow photographer I'd love to learn about what he has seen through his lens. Overall, I'd be interested to learn about his latest hobby or his work in Nepal.
