RICHARD TON GIANG
My name is Richard Ton Giang and I'm the son of Vietnamese refugees. Growing up with the self-perception of being "the other" built up by "minor feelings" led me to cling onto my identity through a simple phrase: Finish Your Food. Over the years this mantra has cultivated three distinct meanings for myself: Take advantage of every opportunity, never forget where I've come from, and always finish what I've started.

Some context.
My parents were forced from their homes in South Vietnam during post-war reconstruction. Along with leaving behind everything they owned they also left behind their identities. All the while, a result of the trauma would slowly take form in an emboldened hatred towards anything that reminded them of what was stripped of them. The culmination of their resentment and erasure of their past would completely shape my upbringing in rural-suburban Ohio.
